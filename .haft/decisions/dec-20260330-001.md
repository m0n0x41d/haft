## 2. Decision

**Selected:** Per-artifact lineage-preserving disposition

All 9 stale/drifted decisions still map to live concerns in the current haft-era codebase, but their affected_files point at pre-refactor quint/plugin-mcp paths. Per-artifact supersession preserves historical intent and auditability without pretending the old baselines still describe the current implementation layout.

**Invariants:**
- Do not deprecate a decision whose underlying concern still exists in current code.
- Preserve lineage explicitly from each stale decision to its replacement artifact.
- Do not claim current implementation is verified solely from historical decision text; refresh noise must be resolved by artifact lifecycle, not by reinterpretation.

**Post-conditions:**
- [ ] A replacement artifact exists for each of the 9 stale/drifted decisions.
- [ ] Each original decision is marked superseded with rationale tied to path/command migration.
- [ ] Refresh is rerun and no longer reports those 9 items as active stale/drift noise.

**Admissibility:**
- NOT: Do not bulk deprecate these decisions as obsolete without evidence the concern is dead.
- NOT: Do not delete legacy artifacts.
- NOT: Do not leave stale active decisions pointing at nonexistent src/mcp paths once replacements are recorded.

**Weakest link:** Successor mapping quality depends on the replacement artifact being explicit enough; vague supersession text would preserve lineage badly and create audit ambiguity.
